When to Choose Each Service

Both are real managed services — not VA staffing platforms. The right choice depends on your business model and where you want to go.

When to Choose Televista

If you want more firepower for less money — that's us. Whether you're chasing distressed properties, pre-foreclosures, absentee owners, or probate leads, our callers are trained to handle all of it. We don't just do motivated sellers — we do motivated sellers better, with AI-powered lead scoring that tells you which ones are most likely to close, n8n and Zapier automations that route hot leads instantly, and a QA team that listens to every call so nothing slips through. You also get daily scorecards, weekly strategy calls, a CallTools power dialer with 100 local numbers, and integrations with every CRM that matters — all starting at $1,250/mo with no contracts. We take it personally when a campaign doesn't perform. That's the Egyptian work ethic — we don't do "good enough."

  • You want better results on motivated seller campaigns at a lower price
  • You need AI lead scoring, automation, and deep CRM integration
  • You want a fully managed operation — not just callers, but the entire pipeline
  • You might expand into other lead types (insurance, solar, B2B) down the road

When to Choose Call Motivated Sellers

Credit where it's due — CMS knows motivated sellers. If your entire world is distressed property acquisition and you want a company that only does that one thing, they're worth a look. Their callers are trained specifically for RE seller conversations, and they integrate with REsimpli and Podio (the two CRMs every wholesaler already uses). It's a focused product for a focused buyer. Not flashy, not trying to be everything — just motivated seller calls. Respect.

  • Your business is exclusively focused on motivated seller acquisition
  • You already use REsimpli or Podio and want tight integration
  • You prefer a provider that specializes in one vertical over breadth of services

The Bottom Line

Both are legit managed cold calling services — not VA staffing where you're doing all the work yourself. The difference is scope and price. Televista gives you more tools, more CRM integrations, AI scoring, automation, and multi-industry coverage at $1,250/mo. CMS is narrower but deeply specialized in RE motivated sellers at ~$1,800/mo.

Here's the honest take: if you want one provider to handle calling, list building, skip tracing, automation, and everything else — or you might expand beyond real estate someday — Televista is the play. If your entire operation is motivated sellers and you want a team that eats, sleeps, and breathes that niche, CMS is solid. Either way, you're better off than hiring a random VA and praying.
