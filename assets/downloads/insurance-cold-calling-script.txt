INSURANCE COLD CALLING SCRIPT — LIFE & P&C AGENTS
====================================================

PURPOSE: Set qualified appointments for policy reviews and new coverage.
Works for independent agents, captive agents, and insurance lead gen teams.


--- OPENING (Life Event Trigger) ---

Option A — Policy Review Hook:
"Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] with [Agency]. I'm reaching out
because we're doing complimentary policy reviews for folks in [City/Area]
this month. A lot of people we talk to find out they're either overpaying or
they have gaps in their coverage they didn't know about. Do you have about
60 seconds for me to ask a couple of quick questions?"

Option B — Life Event Trigger:
"Hi [First Name], congratulations on [the new home / the new baby / the
recent marriage] — this is [Your Name] with [Agency]. Big life changes
like that usually mean your insurance needs have shifted too. I was just
calling to see if you've had a chance to update your coverage, or if
that's been on the back burner."

[If they ask how you know:] "It's public record — I promise I'm not
stalking you. I just like to reach out to folks when there's a good reason
to take a fresh look at their coverage."


--- QUALIFICATION QUESTIONS ---

1. CURRENT COVERAGE:
   "Do you currently have [life/home/auto] insurance? And do you remember
   roughly when you last reviewed your policy — within the last year, or
   has it been a while?"

2. COVERAGE GAPS:
   "A lot of folks I talk to find they're underinsured without realizing it.
   For example, do you have an umbrella policy? What about coverage for
   [income replacement / rental property / business liability]?"

3. SATISFACTION:
   "How happy are you with your current carrier and what you're paying? If
   1 is 'I'm shopping right now' and 10 is 'I'll never leave,' where do
   you fall?"

4. BUDGET:
   "Is your premium something you feel good about, or does it feel like
   it's been creeping up on you?"

5. DECISION-MAKING:
   "When it comes to making a change on your insurance, is that something
   you'd handle yourself, or would you want your [spouse/partner/business
   partner] involved?"


--- OBJECTION HANDLING ---

OBJECTION: "I already have insurance."
RESPONSE: "That's great — most people I call do. I'm not trying to get you
to cancel anything. What I am offering is a free side-by-side comparison.
A lot of times we find we can either save you money on the same coverage,
or get you better coverage for the same price. Would it be worth 15 minutes
to find out? If we can't beat what you have, I'll tell you to keep it."

OBJECTION: "I can't afford it."
RESPONSE: "I totally understand — and that's actually one of the biggest
reasons to do a review. You might be paying for coverage you don't need,
or there might be discounts you're not getting. I've helped people save
$50 to $200 a month just by restructuring their policies. It costs nothing
to look. Can I ask what you're paying now?"

OBJECTION: "Send me something in the mail."
RESPONSE: "I'd be happy to — but to send you something that's actually
useful and not just a generic brochure, I'd need to know a little about
your situation. Can I ask you two quick questions so I can put together
something specific to you? It'll take 30 seconds."

[Then ask about current coverage and what they're paying. Use the answers
to build a personalized quote and follow up within 48 hours.]


--- APPOINTMENT SETTING ---

"Based on what you've told me, I think there's a real opportunity to
[save you money / close that gap in your coverage / make sure your family
is properly protected]. Here's what I'd like to do — I'll pull together
a custom comparison and walk you through it. It takes about 15 to 20
minutes, and there's zero obligation. Would [Day] at [Time] work, or
is [Day] at [Time] better?"

[For life insurance specifically:]
"I'll also run a quick needs analysis so we can see exactly how much
coverage makes sense based on your income, debts, and family situation.
No medical exam required for the quote."

[Confirm:] Full name, best callback number, email for sending the
comparison, and confirm appointment time.


--- DELIVERY TIPS ---

- Insurance is boring to most people. Lead with outcomes: "save money,"
  "protect your family," "close gaps." Not policy jargon.
- Life event triggers outperform cold lists 3-to-1. If you can reference
  a recent home purchase, new baby, or marriage, use it.
- Urgency without pressure: "Rates in your age bracket are actually at a
  good point right now" works better than "This offer expires Friday."
- For life insurance, normalize the conversation: "It's one of those
  things nobody wants to think about, but everyone's glad they handled."
- Always follow up with a personalized email within 1 hour of the call.
