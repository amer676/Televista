COLD CALLING OBJECTION HANDLING CHEAT SHEET
=============================================

Top 15 objections with word-for-word responses. Print this out
and keep it next to your phone.


---------------------------------------------

OBJECTION: "I'm not interested."
RESPONSE: "I totally get it — you weren't expecting this call. I'm not
asking you to be interested yet. If I could show you [specific benefit]
in under 2 minutes, would it at least be worth hearing? If not, I'll
let you go."
WHY IT WORKS: Reframes "not interested" from a final answer to a
premature judgment — most people will give you the 2 minutes.

---------------------------------------------

OBJECTION: "Now's not a good time."
RESPONSE: "No problem — I want to respect your time. When would be a
better time to have a quick 2-minute conversation? I can call you back
at a specific time that works."
WHY IT WORKS: Treats the objection as a scheduling issue (which it
usually is) rather than a rejection, and pins down a callback time.

---------------------------------------------

OBJECTION: "How did you get my number?"
RESPONSE: "Good question — your [property/business/name] came up on a
list we use to reach people who might benefit from [what you offer]. I
know it's unexpected, but I'm a real person and I'll be quick. Can I
ask you one question?"
WHY IT WORKS: Acknowledges the concern honestly, humanizes you, and
redirects to a low-commitment ask.

---------------------------------------------

OBJECTION: "I'm on the Do Not Call list."
RESPONSE: "I appreciate you letting me know — I'll make sure to remove
your number from our list right away. Before I do, can I ask one quick
question just so I have the right information to take you off?"
WHY IT WORKS: Shows immediate compliance and respect, which often
disarms the hostility and opens a brief window for conversation.

---------------------------------------------

OBJECTION: "I'm already working with someone."
RESPONSE: "That's great — glad you're taken care of. Just out of
curiosity, how's that going? Is there anything you wish was different
about the experience?"
WHY IT WORKS: Doesn't challenge their current choice, but opens the
door to dissatisfaction they may not have voiced yet.

---------------------------------------------

OBJECTION: "It's too expensive."
RESPONSE: "I hear you — and price definitely matters. Can I ask what
you're comparing it to? A lot of times when I break down the actual
cost versus [what they're losing/spending now], the math actually works
in your favor."
WHY IT WORKS: Shifts from sticker shock to value comparison, which is
where you win the conversation.

---------------------------------------------

OBJECTION: "I'm not selling / I'm not looking to buy."
RESPONSE: "Totally understand — and I'm not trying to pressure you into
anything. Just curious though — is there a price or a situation that
would change your mind? Sometimes people have a number in their head."
WHY IT WORKS: Gets them thinking about conditions rather than a flat
no. People almost always have a number.

---------------------------------------------

OBJECTION: "I just bought it / I just signed a contract."
RESPONSE: "Congrats on that — sounds like good timing. How about this:
I'll make a note to check back in [6 months / when their contract is
up]. Would that be alright? And what would be the best number to
reach you?"
WHY IT WORKS: Positions you as patient and respectful, and secures
permission for a warm follow-up later.

---------------------------------------------

OBJECTION: "Just send me some information."
RESPONSE: "Happy to — but I want to send you something that's actually
relevant, not a generic brochure. Can I ask you two quick questions so
I can tailor it to your situation? It'll take 30 seconds."
WHY IT WORKS: Turns a brush-off into a mini-qualification call. Once
they answer two questions, they're engaged.

---------------------------------------------

OBJECTION: "Call me back some other time."
RESPONSE: "Absolutely — I don't want to play phone tag though. What
day and time works best for you? I'll put it on my calendar and call
you right on the dot."
WHY IT WORKS: Forces specificity. "Some other time" is code for
"never" unless you pin down a date.

---------------------------------------------

OBJECTION: "I need to talk to my spouse / partner first."
RESPONSE: "Makes total sense — you should absolutely make this decision
together. What if we set up a time when both of you are available? That
way I can answer any questions either of you might have. Would an
evening or weekend work better?"
WHY IT WORKS: Includes the spouse as a participant rather than letting
them become an invisible objection you can never address.

---------------------------------------------

OBJECTION: "What's the catch?"
RESPONSE: "Fair question — no catch. Here's what we do: [brief, plain-
English explanation]. We make money when [explain your business model
simply]. If it doesn't work for you, you walk away. No contracts, no
pressure. Does that make sense?"
WHY IT WORKS: Radical transparency kills skepticism. People are used
to being dodged, so a straight answer builds instant trust.

---------------------------------------------

OBJECTION: "I don't do business over the phone."
RESPONSE: "I respect that — a lot of people feel the same way. This call
is just to see if it makes sense to meet in person or set up a video
call. I'm not asking you to commit to anything right now. Would a
15-minute meeting work better for you?"
WHY IT WORKS: Validates their preference and repositions the call as a
scheduling step, not a transaction.

---------------------------------------------

OBJECTION: "Is this a scam?"
RESPONSE: "Ha — I get why you'd ask that, and I'm glad you're cautious.
Here's who we are: [Company name], based in [City]. You can Google us,
check our reviews, and I'll send you my direct info right after this
call. I'm a real person and I'm happy to prove it. Can I ask you one
quick question?"
WHY IT WORKS: Laughing it off lightly (not defensively) plus offering
verifiable details shows you have nothing to hide.

---------------------------------------------

OBJECTION: "I'm busy right now."
RESPONSE: "I'll be quick — 60 seconds, and if it's not relevant, I'm
gone. [Deliver your fastest, tightest pitch.] Does that sound like
something worth a longer conversation, or should I let you go?"
WHY IT WORKS: Respects their time constraint while still delivering
your hook. Most people can spare 60 seconds.

---------------------------------------------


GENERAL RULES FOR HANDLING ANY OBJECTION:

1. Never argue. Agree first, then redirect.
2. Use "just out of curiosity" and "quick question" to keep things low
   pressure.
3. An objection is not a rejection — it's a request for more information
   or a test to see if you'll fold.
4. If you get the same objection three times in a row from the same
   person, respect it and move on.
5. Track which objections you hear most often. If more than 30% of your
   calls hit the same wall, your opening needs work.
