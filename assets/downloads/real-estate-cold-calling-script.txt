REAL ESTATE MOTIVATED SELLER COLD CALLING SCRIPT
=================================================

PURPOSE: Generate appointments with homeowners who may be motivated to sell
their property below market value. Works for wholesalers, investors, and
acquisition teams.


--- OPENING (Pattern Interrupt) ---

"Hey [First Name], this is [Your Name] — I'm reaching out because I noticed
your property on [Street Name]. I work with a group of local buyers, and
honestly, I just had a quick question for you. Do you have about 60 seconds?"

[If they say yes, continue. If they ask what this is about:]

"Totally fair — I'm not trying to sell you anything. We buy houses in the
[City/County] area, and your property came up on our list. I was just curious
if you've given any thought to selling, even if it's down the road."


--- QUALIFICATION QUESTIONS ---

Ask these conversationally. Do NOT interrogate. Listen more than you talk.

1. MOTIVATION:
   "What's your current situation with the property? Are you living there,
   renting it out, or is it sitting vacant?"

   [If renting:] "How's that going for you? Keeping you up at night or
   running pretty smooth?"

2. TIMELINE:
   "If you did decide to sell, is there a timeframe you'd be working with —
   like something in the next few weeks, or more of a 'maybe someday' thing?"

3. PROPERTY CONDITION:
   "On a scale of 1 to 10, how would you rate the condition? Any major
   repairs you've been putting off — roof, foundation, HVAC, anything
   like that?"

4. PRICE FLEXIBILITY:
   "Have you had the property appraised recently, or do you have a ballpark
   number in mind for what you'd want to walk away with?"

   [If they give a number:] "Got it. And is there any flexibility there, or
   is that pretty firm?"

5. MORTGAGE/LIENS:
   "Do you still owe anything on it, or is it free and clear?"


--- OBJECTION HANDLING ---

OBJECTION: "I'm not interested."
RESPONSE: "I completely understand — most people aren't when I first call.
I'm not asking you to commit to anything. If I could make you a no-obligation
cash offer within 24 hours, would it at least be worth hearing? If it doesn't
work for you, no hard feelings."

OBJECTION: "How did you get my number?"
RESPONSE: "Great question — your property showed up on a list we use to find
homeowners who might benefit from a cash offer. I know it's a little
unexpected, but I promise this isn't a robocall situation. I'm a real person
and I'll be quick. Is there any chance you've thought about selling?"

OBJECTION: "I'm already working with someone."
RESPONSE: "No problem at all — are you listed with an agent, or are you
talking to another investor? [If agent:] How long have you been on the
market? [If investor:] Have they actually made you a written offer yet?
Sometimes it helps to have a backup option."

OBJECTION: "What are you going to offer me?"
RESPONSE: "Honestly, I can't throw out a number without seeing the property
and running the numbers. What I can tell you is we close fast — usually 2 to
3 weeks — and we cover all the closing costs. No commissions, no repairs
needed. Would it be worth 15 minutes for me to come take a look and get you
a real number?"

OBJECTION: "I'm not selling."
RESPONSE: "Totally respect that. Just out of curiosity — is there a price
that would change your mind? I'm not trying to pressure you. Sometimes people
have a 'magic number' and if we can hit it, great. If not, I'll take you off
my list."


--- CLOSING / APPOINTMENT SETTING ---

"It sounds like this might be worth exploring. Here's what I'd like to do —
I'd love to swing by the property, take a quick look, and put together a
real offer for you within 24 hours. No obligation, no pressure. Would
[Day] at [Time] or [Day] at [Time] work better for you?"

[If they hesitate:] "I get it — let me do this. I'll put together some
preliminary numbers based on what you've told me and send those over.
If the range looks good, we can schedule a quick walkthrough. What's the
best email or number to reach you?"


--- NOTES ON TONE AND DELIVERY ---

- Speak like a human, not a telemarketer. Contractions, pauses, and
  conversational filler ("honestly," "just," "real quick") build trust.
- Match their energy. If they're quiet, slow down. If they're chatty,
  let them talk — motivated sellers often tell you everything you need
  to know if you just listen.
- Never argue. If they say no, agree with them. "I totally get it" is
  your most powerful phrase.
- Smile when you talk. It changes your vocal tone more than you think.
- Stand up while calling. It gives you more energy and confidence.
- Your goal is NOT to close a deal on this call. Your goal is to get
  an appointment or permission to follow up.
- Log every call in your CRM immediately. Notes on motivation level,
  timeline, and property condition are gold for follow-ups.
