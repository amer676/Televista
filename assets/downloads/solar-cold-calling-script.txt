SOLAR COLD CALLING SCRIPT — RESIDENTIAL HOMEOWNERS
====================================================

PURPOSE: Set qualified appointments for solar consultations and roof
inspections. Works for solar installers, dealers, and lead gen teams.


--- OPENING ---

"Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. I'm calling because
homeowners in [City/Neighborhood] have been getting hit with some pretty
steep utility increases lately — some folks are seeing 20 to 30 percent
higher bills this year. I was curious if you've looked into solar at all,
or if that's something you'd be open to hearing about for 60 seconds?"

[If they seem receptive:]
"Great — and just so you know, I'm not going to try to sell you anything
on this call. I'm just trying to figure out if your home might be a good
fit for a program we're running in your area."


--- QUALIFICATION QUESTIONS ---

1. HOMEOWNERSHIP:
   "First question — you do own the home, correct?"
   [If renting: "Got it — unfortunately this program is only for homeowners.
   I appreciate your time."]

2. ROOF AGE:
   "Do you have a rough idea of how old your roof is? Has it been replaced
   in the last 15 years or so?"
   [If roof is 20+ years: flag for potential roof replacement bundled deal]

3. SHADE AND ORIENTATION:
   "Is your roof pretty open to sunlight, or do you have a lot of trees
   or tall buildings nearby that shade it during the day?"

4. ELECTRIC BILL:
   "Roughly what are you paying for electricity each month? Over $150?
   Over $200?"
   [Ideal: $150+ monthly. Below $100 is typically not a strong candidate.]

5. CREDIT:
   "Most of our homeowners go with a $0-down financing option. Would you
   say your credit is in the good-to-excellent range — generally above
   680 or so?"
   [Handle gently — don't push if they're uncomfortable.]


--- OBJECTION HANDLING ---

OBJECTION: "Solar is too expensive."
RESPONSE: "I hear that a lot, and honestly it used to be true. But the way
most of our homeowners do it now, there's zero out-of-pocket cost. You're
basically replacing your electric bill with a lower solar payment — so your
monthly cost actually goes down from day one. The consultation is free, and
there's no obligation. Would it be worth 20 minutes to see the actual
numbers for your specific home?"

OBJECTION: "I'm locked into a contract with my utility."
RESPONSE: "That's actually one of the best things about solar — you're not
breaking any contract. You stay connected to the grid, and the utility
company is still there as your backup. Solar just reduces what you pull from
them, which shrinks your bill. There's no switching or cancellation involved."

OBJECTION: "I don't trust solar companies."
RESPONSE: "I completely understand — there are some companies out there that
have given the industry a bad look. That's exactly why we do a no-pressure
in-home consultation first. Our installer handles all the permits, the
warranty is 25 years, and we don't move forward unless the numbers genuinely
make sense for you. Would it help to just see the proposal with no
commitment?"

OBJECTION: "I'm renting."
RESPONSE: "Got it — this particular program is designed for homeowners since
the panels go on the roof. But if you know the property owner, we'd be happy
to reach out to them. Do you happen to have their contact info?"


--- APPOINTMENT SETTING ---

"Here's what I'd like to do — we have a solar consultant in your area this
week who can do a free roof analysis and put together a custom savings
proposal. It takes about 20 minutes and there's absolutely no obligation.
Would [Day] in the morning or afternoon work better for you?"

[Confirm:] "Perfect. I've got you down for [Day] at [Time]. You'll get a
confirmation text from us. The consultant's name is [Name]. They'll take a
quick look at your roof, pull up your utility data, and show you exactly
what you'd save. Sound good?"

[Get:] Full name, property address, email, confirm phone number, and
monthly electric bill amount for the consultant's pre-work.


--- DELIVERY TIPS ---

- Lead with savings, not technology. Homeowners don't care about kilowatts
  and inverters. They care about paying less.
- Use "homeowners in your area" framing — it creates social proof and makes
  the call feel less targeted.
- If they mention a spouse, pivot to: "It would actually be great to have
  both of you there so you can make the decision together."
- Never bash their current utility. Position solar as an addition, not
  a replacement.
