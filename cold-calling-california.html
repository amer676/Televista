The Problem

Why California Eats Investors Alive

High property values mean massive profit potential — but breaking through in the Golden State takes more than a skip trace list and a phone. Here's what stops most California investors from scaling:

01

Hyper-Competitive Market

Every motivated seller in California is being targeted by dozens of investors, wholesalers, and iBuyers simultaneously. Direct mail, text blasts, and PPC ads flood the market. The only way to cut through the noise is live, human conversation — and most investors don't have the bandwidth to do it themselves.

02

Astronomical Lead Costs

Pay-per-lead services in California charge a premium because of high property values. A single qualified seller lead in Los Angeles can cost $200–$500 through paid channels. Without a consistent outbound engine, you're either overpaying for leads or going without them entirely.

03

Massive Territory to Cover

California stretches over 163,000 square miles with 58 counties and hundreds of micro-markets. From coastal San Diego to the Central Valley to NorCal suburbs, each area has different seller motivations. You can't blanket the state alone — you need a system that scales.

FAQ

California Cold Calling Questions

How does Televista generate real estate leads in California?

Televista assigns trained cold callers to your California target markets. They dial homeowners in your chosen zip codes across Los Angeles, San Diego, Sacramento, the Inland Empire, or any California county. When a motivated seller is identified, you receive the lead immediately with full call notes and property details.

Which California markets can you cover?

We cover every California market from the Oregon border to San Diego. Popular target areas include Los Angeles County, the Inland Empire (Riverside and San Bernardino), Sacramento metro, San Diego County, the Bay Area, Fresno, Bakersfield, and the Central Valley. You choose the zip codes and we build your campaign around them.

Is cold calling legal for real estate in California?

Yes. Cold calling for real estate is legal in California when done in compliance with federal TCPA regulations and state telemarketing laws. Televista uses only live human callers, honors the Do Not Call registry, calls within permitted hours, and follows all California-specific consumer protection requirements.

How do you handle California's competitive real estate market?

Volume and speed are key in California. Our callers make hundreds of dials daily across your target areas, reaching homeowners before they list or respond to mail campaigns. We focus on distressed situations — pre-foreclosure, probate, divorce, code violations — where sellers need solutions, not just another offer.

What kind of ROI can California investors expect from cold calling?

California's high property values mean larger assignment fees and profit margins per deal. A single wholesale deal in Los Angeles or San Diego can net $20,000 to $50,000 or more. With Televista plans starting at $1,750 per month, one closed deal can return 10x or more on your investment in outbound calling.
