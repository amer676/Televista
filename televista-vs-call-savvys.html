Televista vs Call Savvys:
Which Team Actually Scales With You?

Last updated: June 2026

Call Savvys is a small, dedicated-caller cold calling shop — and credit where it's due, they say the right things: dedicated callers, no third-party call centers. But "a caller or two on the phone" and "a full pipeline operation that scales" are different products. We put Televista and Call Savvys side by side — team size, technology, CRM integration, reporting, and how much you have to take on faith. We're the premium option here, not the cheapest. When to Choose Each

Call Savvys isn't a bad operator. The question is whether a small shop gives you everything you need — and how much of it you're taking on their word.

When to Choose Televista

You want a premium, full-stack operation that scales — not just a caller or two. Televista is for real estate investors, solar teams, roofers, insurance, and B2B companies who want the whole machine: a 25-person calling team with 10 on standby, AI lead scoring that actually prioritizes your hottest leads, integration with whatever CRM you already run (GoHighLevel, HubSpot, Salesforce, REsimpli, Podio — we adapt to your stack instead of forcing ours on you), n8n and Zapier automation, weekly strategy calls, and a real-time dashboard so you're never guessing what your money bought. Look at our case studies for what that looks like in practice. We're the premium option, and we earn it.

When to Choose Call Savvys

Fair is fair: Call Savvys advertises dedicated callers and says it doesn't outsource to third-party call centers, and their entry price is lower than ours. If you're testing the water with a single caller on a tight budget and you're comfortable working inside their proprietary Pipelio CRM, they can be a reasonable starting point. Their plans are simple — one, two, or three callers, estimated lead ranges, month to month. Just go in clear-eyed: it's a small operation with a limited public track record, the lead numbers are estimates, and you're taking a fair amount on faith. For a low-commitment first test, that may be fine.

The Bottom Line

Both Televista and Call Savvys put dedicated callers on the phone — that part's genuinely similar. The difference is everything around the call. Televista brings a 25-caller team with built-in redundancy, AI lead scoring, deep CRM integration with no lock-in, n8n and Zapier automation, weekly strategy sessions, a real-time dashboard, and a track record you can actually inspect. Call Savvys is a smaller, simpler shop — lower entry price, one to three callers, their own CRM, estimated lead ranges, and a thinner public footprint. If you want the cheapest way to get a caller dialing, they're worth a look. If you want a premium partner that scores, routes, automates, and scales your pipeline — and shows you the receipts — that's Televista.
