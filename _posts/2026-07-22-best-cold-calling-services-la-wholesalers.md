## Key Strategies and Best Practices

Before you hand a list to any caller — in-house or outsourced — the strategy underneath that call has to be solid. A bad script with a great dialer is still a bad call.

**Start with your list hygiene.** LA has a massive, messy pool of property owners, and calling stale or unqualified data wastes everyone's time. Pull lists through [BatchLeads](https://www.batchleads.io) or [PropStream](https://www.propstream.com) and filter by equity, absentee status, and pre-foreclosure flags before a single dial goes out. Most wholesalers skip this step and then wonder why their connect-to-appointment rate is garbage.

Dialer setup matters more than people admit. A predictive dialer like [Readymode](https://www.readymode.com) — which services like [VA Horizon](https://www.vahorizon.site/compare/best-real-estate-cold-calling-services) include as part of their package — keeps your callers in conversations instead of waiting on rings. Dead air kills momentum fast.

> **Pro tip:** Don't let your callers freestyle the qualification. Lock down your script to hit the four things that actually matter — motivation, timeline, condition, and whether they've got any liens. Everything else is small talk.

**Script structure for the LA market specifically:**

- Open with a local angle — mention the neighborhood, not just "your property"
- Disqualify fast — don't waste 8 minutes on a seller who wants retail
- Anchor the appointment, don't ask for permission — "I'd like to get someone out there Tuesday or Wednesday, which works better?" beats "would it be okay if..."
- Keep objection handlers short and conversational

CRM integration is the piece that falls apart most often. If your callers are logging notes in a spreadsheet and you're manually moving leads into [REsimpli](https://www.resimpli.com) or [HighLevel](https://www.gohighlevel.com), you're bleeding follow-up. Services that bundle CRM setup — [VA Horizon](https://www.vahorizon.site/compare/best-real-estate-cold-calling-services), for example, includes [HighLevel CRM setup](https://www.vahorizon.site/compare/best-real-estate-cold-calling-services) automatically — cut that friction down considerably.

TCPA compliance in California isn't optional. State laws run stricter than federal baseline, so whoever's dialing for you needs to be scrubbing against the national DNC list and honoring California-specific restrictions. Ask any service you evaluate how they handle this — if they hesitate, walk away.

Weekly QA on call recordings is how you actually improve. [VA Horizon](https://www.vahorizon.site/compare/best-real-estate-cold-calling-services) builds weekly quality assurance into their model, which gives you visibility into what's happening on calls rather than just hoping the numbers come back clean.

The outsourced route — services like [Televista](/services.html), Call Motivated Sellers, REVA Global, and others active in this space — works best when you treat the caller team as an extension of your operation, not a black box you pay and ignore. Stay in the weekly reviews. Adjust the script when the data tells you to.

## Step-by-Step Implementation

You've picked a service. Now what? Most wholesalers skip straight to "start the calls" and wonder why nothing converts. Don't do that.

**Step 1: Clean your list before anyone dials a single number.**

Pull your distressed seller data through [BatchLeads](https://www.batchleads.io) or [PropStream](https://www.propstream.com) — filter by equity percentage, absentee owners, tax delinquency, and probate flags. LA lists get stale fast. Bad data = burned caller time = zero pipeline.

**Step 2: Confirm TCPA compliance before launch.**

California is aggressive about this. Your service needs to be scrubbing against the DNC registry and using TCPA-compliant dialing practices — not just telling you they do it. Ask specifically how they handle cell phone consent in CA. Get it in writing.

**Step 3: Set up your CRM to catch every lead.**

If you're running outsourced calls through a managed service, make sure their dispositions are flowing into a CRM you actually control. [REsimpli](https://www.resimpli.com) works well for wholesalers; [HighLevel](https://www.gohighlevel.com) is what some services bundle in directly — [VA Horizon](https://www.vahorizon.site/compare/best-real-estate-cold-calling-services), for instance, includes a HighLevel CRM setup as part of their package alongside [Readymode](https://www.readymode.com) dialer access and weekly QA. Not a bad setup if you want things pre-built.

> **Pro tip:** Don't just track "qualified leads" — track conversation rate and lead-to-appointment rate separately. You'll quickly see whether your caller, your script, or your list is the weak link.

**Step 4: Brief your callers on the script — and your criteria.**

What makes a motivated seller in your LA market? Pre-foreclosure? Out-of-state owner? Probate? Your callers should know the difference between a soft lead and a real appointment. Generic scripts don't work here.

**Step 5: Run the first two weeks as a test, not a commitment.**

Managed cold calling services for wholesalers vary wildly in call quality. A service like [Televista](/services.html) handles the full campaign — callers, scripts, follow-up — but regardless of who you use, [book a strategy call](/contact.html) first, set a 30-day benchmark, and measure relentlessly before scaling spend.

Adjust. Then scale.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

Most wholesalers running cold calling campaigns in LA make the same handful of mistakes. Repeatedly.

**Hiring based on price alone.** A cheaper caller who doesn't understand California's distressed seller psychology — or who's reading a generic script — is going to burn through your list and produce nothing. I've seen this pattern more times than I'd like. You get 300 dials, 4 callbacks, and zero appointments. The list wasn't the problem.

**Skipping TCPA compliance entirely.** California's enforcement environment isn't forgiving, and the LA market gets scrutiny. If your calling service can't tell you exactly how they're handling DNC scrubbing and consent, that's a hard pass. Every legitimate service — whether it's [Televista](/services.html), [Call Porter](https://callporter.com), or anyone else — should have a clear compliance process before a single dial goes out.

**Not checking QA until something breaks.** Services like [VA Horizon](https://www.vahorizon.site/compare/best-real-estate-cold-calling-services) offer weekly quality assurance as part of their model — which is the right idea. Waiting until your calendar is empty to audit call recordings is backwards.

> **Pro tip:** Pull 10 random call recordings every week, not 10 at the end of the month. Problems compound fast when nobody's listening.

**Ignoring CRM setup before launch.** If your caller books a lead and there's nowhere clean to log it — no [REsimpli](https://www.resimpli.com) pipeline, no [HubSpot](https://www.hubspot.com) workflow — that lead dies in a spreadsheet. Setup first, calls second.

**Letting the list go stale.** LA ownership data moves fast. Pull fresh data from [BatchLeads](https://www.batchleads.io) regularly. Most people refresh quarterly at best. That's too slow.
