Every real estate investing strategy starts with data. Whether you are wholesaling, flipping, or building a rental portfolio, the quality of your lead list determines the quality of your results. You can have the best cold callers in the world, the most compelling direct mail piece ever written, and a closing process refined over thousands of deals, but if your data is wrong, stale, or poorly targeted, none of it matters.

The data platform landscape for real estate investors has expanded dramatically. What used to require trips to the county courthouse and hours of manual research can now be done in minutes from your laptop. Platforms like PropStream, BatchLeads, and ListSource have become essential tools for investors at every level. But they are not all the same, and choosing the right one for your operation can save you thousands of dollars in wasted marketing spend.

This guide compares the top data sources for real estate lead lists, breaks down their features, and shows you how to use them to build lists that actually convert.

## Key Takeaways

- PropStream is the most feature-rich platform for real estate investors and is ideal for list building, comps, and marketing
- BatchLeads excels at combining list building with skip tracing and outreach in a single platform
- ListSource from CoreLogic is best for large-volume list pulls with granular demographic filtering
- Stacking multiple data criteria dramatically improves list quality and conversion rates
- Skip tracing quality varies significantly between providers, so test multiple services
- Refreshing your data every 60-90 days prevents wasted effort on stale records

## PropStream

PropStream has become the go-to platform for a large portion of the real estate investing community, and for good reason. It combines property data, ownership information, comparable sales, mortgage details, and marketing tools in a single interface.

### Key Features

**Nationwide property data**: PropStream covers every county in the United States with property records sourced from county assessors, recorders, and multiple listing services. You can search by address, owner name, or geographic area.

**Advanced list building**: The filter system is where PropStream really shines. You can build lists using dozens of criteria including:

- Absentee owners
- High-equity properties (customizable equity threshold)
- Pre-foreclosures and auction properties
- Tax delinquent properties
- Vacant properties
- Owner-occupied versus investor-owned
- Property type (single family, multi-family, land, commercial)
- Year built, square footage, and bedroom/bathroom count
- Ownership duration
- Liens and judgments

**List stacking**: PropStream allows you to apply multiple filters simultaneously, which is critical for building high-quality lists. A list of absentee owners with high equity, long ownership duration, and out-of-state mailing addresses will always outperform a list with a single filter.

**Comparable sales analysis**: You can run comps directly within PropStream, which is valuable when evaluating potential deals. The comp tool pulls recent sales, active listings, and pending sales in the area.

**Skip tracing**: PropStream offers built-in skip tracing starting at around $0.12-$0.15 per record. The quality is decent for basic campaigns, though some investors prefer dedicated skip tracing services for better hit rates.

**Marketing tools**: PropStream includes direct mail and email marketing features, allowing you to go from list building to outreach without leaving the platform.

### Pricing

PropStream is priced at $99 per month for an individual subscription. This includes access to the full property database and list building tools. Skip tracing, marketing features, and some advanced tools carry additional per-record costs.

### Best For

PropStream is ideal for investors who want an all-in-one platform for research, list building, comps, and marketing. It is particularly strong for wholesalers and flippers who need to evaluate deals quickly and pull targeted lists regularly.

## BatchLeads

BatchLeads has carved out a strong position in the market by focusing on the intersection of data and outreach. While PropStream leans more toward research and analysis, BatchLeads is built for action. It is designed to help you go from list to contact as quickly as possible.

### Key Features

**List building with motivation indicators**: BatchLeads provides standard property and ownership filters plus proprietary motivation scoring. The platform flags properties with multiple distress indicators, helping you prioritize the leads most likely to convert.

**Integrated skip tracing**: One of BatchLeads' strongest advantages is its built-in skip tracing. You can pull a list and skip trace it in the same workflow, often within seconds. Their skip tracing rates are competitive, typically in the $0.10-$0.15 per record range.

**Driving for dollars integration**: The BatchLeads mobile app allows you to drive neighborhoods, photograph distressed properties, and add them directly to your lead lists for skip tracing and outreach.

**Direct outreach tools**: BatchLeads includes direct mail, SMS, and email features so you can reach your leads without exporting data to another platform. The text messaging feature supports both manual texting and drip campaigns.

**Property comp tool**: Like PropStream, BatchLeads offers comparable sales data for evaluating deals.

**Team features**: BatchLeads supports multiple users under a single account with role-based permissions, making it suitable for growing teams.

### Pricing

BatchLeads pricing starts at around $79 per month for the base plan, with higher tiers that include more records, skip traces, and outreach features. The pricing structure is usage-based for many features, so costs scale with volume.

### Best For

BatchLeads is ideal for investors who want a streamlined workflow from list building to outreach. It is particularly strong for operations that rely heavily on cold calling and texting, since you can go from data pull to phone contact in minutes.

## ListSource

ListSource, powered by CoreLogic, is one of the oldest and most established data sources in the real estate industry. CoreLogic is the same company that provides data to major real estate platforms and financial institutions, which gives ListSource access to one of the deepest property data sets available.

### Key Features

**Extensive filtering options**: ListSource offers granular filtering that goes beyond what many newer platforms provide. You can filter by property characteristics, owner demographics, financial data, and geographic boundaries drawn on a map.

**Demographic data**: Unlike PropStream and BatchLeads, which focus primarily on property data, ListSource includes owner demographic information such as estimated income, age, and household composition. This is valuable for targeted campaigns like probate mailings or campaigns focused on specific age groups.

**Large-volume list pulls**: ListSource is built to handle high-volume data requests. If you are pulling 10,000 or more records per month for direct mail or cold calling campaigns, ListSource can handle it efficiently.

**Count before you buy**: ListSource lets you see how many records match your criteria before purchasing, which helps with budgeting and campaign planning.

**Suppression lists**: You can upload your existing leads and suppress them from new pulls, preventing duplicate records and wasted spend.

### Pricing

ListSource uses a per-record pricing model rather than a monthly subscription. Prices vary based on the data selected and the volume of records, typically ranging from $0.03 to $0.10 per record. This can be more cost-effective for large one-time pulls but more expensive over time compared to subscription models for frequent users.

### Best For

ListSource is best for investors who need large-volume data pulls with demographic filtering or who prefer a pay-per-record model instead of a monthly subscription. It is particularly useful for direct mail campaigns where you need to pull and segment tens of thousands of records.

## Other Notable Data Sources

### DealMachine

DealMachine started as a driving-for-dollars app and has expanded into a broader lead generation platform. Its core strength remains the mobile app for identifying distressed properties in the field. It includes skip tracing and direct mail features. Pricing starts at around $49 per month.

### PropertyRadar

PropertyRadar is popular in Western states and offers strong mapping and geographic targeting features. It excels at identifying properties within specific neighborhoods or along specific routes. Its data depth is strongest in California, Oregon, Washington, and a growing number of other states.

### DataTree by First American

DataTree provides property data, title history, and lien information sourced from First American Financial Corporation. It is particularly strong for title research and due diligence rather than marketing list building.

### County Records Direct

Pulling records directly from county assessor and recorder websites is free but time-intensive. This approach works for small-scale operations or for supplementing data from paid platforms. Some counties offer bulk data exports for a fee.

## How to Build a High-Converting Lead List

Regardless of which platform you choose, the list-building methodology remains the same. Here is a step-by-step process for creating lists that produce results.

### Step 1: Define Your Buying Criteria

Before pulling a single record, clarify what types of properties you want to buy. Consider:

- **Property type**: Single family, multi-family, land
- **Price range**: What ARV range are you targeting?
- **Geography**: Which counties, cities, or zip codes?
- **Condition**: Distressed properties only, or any condition?

### Step 2: Identify Motivation Indicators

Stack multiple criteria to target owners who are most likely motivated to sell. The more indicators you stack, the smaller but more motivated your list becomes.

Common motivation stacking combinations:

- Absentee owner + high equity + long ownership (10+ years)
- Pre-foreclosure + owner-occupied + low equity
- Tax delinquent + vacant property + code violations
- Out-of-state owner + high equity + inherited property

### Step 3: Pull and Deduplicate

Pull your list and remove duplicates. If you are using multiple data sources, cross-reference them to ensure you are not paying for the same records twice. Most platforms offer deduplication features.

### Step 4: Skip Trace

Append phone numbers and email addresses to your list. Use a reputable skip tracing service and request multiple phone numbers per record when available. Hit rates of 70-80% are typical for good providers.

### Step 5: Segment for Outreach

Divide your list into segments based on your outreach strategy:

- **Cold calling list**: Records with valid phone numbers
- **Direct mail list**: All records, since you can mail to the property or mailing address
- **Text message list**: Records with mobile numbers (ensure compliance with TCPA)

### Step 6: Launch and Track

Deploy your campaign and track results by list segment. This allows you to identify which data criteria and sources produce the best leads, so you can refine future list pulls.

## Skip Tracing: A Closer Look

Skip tracing is the process of finding current contact information for property owners. The quality of your skip tracing directly impacts your cold calling campaign performance.

### Factors That Affect Skip Tracing Quality

**Data freshness**: Phone numbers change frequently. A skip tracing service that updates its database monthly will outperform one that updates quarterly.

**Number of phone numbers returned**: The best services return three to five phone numbers per record, increasing the odds of reaching the owner.

**Phone type identification**: Services that identify whether a number is a cell phone, landline, or VoIP help you allocate your outreach appropriately. Cell numbers are best for texting, while landlines are fine for cold calling.

### Top Skip Tracing Services

- **BatchSkipTracing**: Popular among investors for its accuracy and competitive pricing
- **REISkip**: Focused specifically on real estate investor use cases
- **TLOxp from TransUnion**: Higher cost but among the most accurate available
- **Built-in skip tracing**: PropStream and BatchLeads both offer integrated options

## Data Hygiene and Maintenance

Your data degrades over time. Phone numbers change, owners sell, and circumstances shift. Maintaining your data is an ongoing process, not a one-time task.

**Refresh skip tracing every 60-90 days**: Reappend phone numbers to your existing lists to capture updated contact information.

**Remove converted leads**: Once a lead has been acquired or marked as permanently uninterested, remove them from active calling lists.

**Update DNC records**: Maintain an internal Do Not Call list and suppress those records from all outreach.

**Track data source performance**: Monitor which platform and criteria combinations produce the highest contact rates and conversion rates. Double down on what works and cut what does not.

## Your Next Move
The best data source for your real estate lead lists depends on your operation's size, strategy, and budget. PropStream is the most comprehensive all-in-one platform and the best starting point for most investors. BatchLeads is the strongest option if your primary outreach method is cold calling and texting and you want everything in one workflow. ListSource is the right choice for high-volume direct mail campaigns that require demographic filtering.

Whichever platform you choose, remember that data is only as good as what you do with it. A well-targeted list of 500 records will outperform a random list of 5,000 every time. Stack your criteria, invest in quality skip tracing, refresh your data regularly, and track your results so you can continuously improve. The difference between a profitable campaign and a losing one often comes down to the data it was built on.
