## Step-by-Step Implementation

Getting your outbound stack running in San Diego doesn't require a six-figure budget or a team of 10. You need the right sequence — because order matters more than most investors realize.

**Step 1: Pick your lead types first.** Don't buy a dialer and then figure out what to call. Start with [REDX](https://www.redx.com)'s segmented data — [Expired Leads](https://www.redx.com/case-studies), [FSBOs](https://www.redx.com/case-studies), and [FRBOs](https://www.redx.com/case-studies) each require a different script and a different pitch. Mixing them into one undifferentiated list is how you waste a week.

**Step 2: Layer in geo-targeted skip tracing.** REDX's [GeoLeads™](https://www.redx.com/case-studies) lets you circle-prospect around a recent deal or a target neighborhood — which is genuinely useful in San Diego where deal density clusters by zip code. Pull the data before you set up your dialer, not after.

**Step 3: Load into a power dialer and build your script.** [REDX's Power Dialer](https://www.redx.com/case-studies) is purpose-built for this workflow. If you're running a higher-volume operation, [Mojo Dialer](https://www.mojosells.com) or [CallTools](https://www.calltools.com) work well too. Your script should match the lead type — an expired listing owner needs a completely different opener than a landlord sitting on an FRBO.

> **Pro tip:** Don't write one script for everything. I've seen callers tank entire sessions because they used a seller script on a landlord. Write three separate openers, test them for a week, then cut the one that's dragging your connect rate down.

**Step 4: Handle follow-up in a CRM immediately.** Load appointments and callbacks into [REsimpli](https://resimpli.com) or [HubSpot](https://www.hubspot.com) the same day. Same-day entry or the lead dies.

**Step 5: Analyze lead intent — not just volume.** [Conversion Realtor](https://conversionrealtor.com/conversion-research/real-estate-conversion-rate-benchmark) turns lead messages into full intelligence reports with intent scores and behavioral signals, so you're not guessing which callbacks to prioritize.

Honestly, most investors skip step five entirely. That's the gap.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

Most investors don't fail at cold calling because they picked the wrong dialer. They fail because they skipped the setup work entirely and wondered why nothing converted.

**Mistake #1: Buying a dialer before you have clean data.** A power dialer calling garbage numbers is just a faster way to waste your morning. Before you touch [REDX](https://www.redx.com)'s Power Dialer or any other tool, make sure your list is segmented — Expired Leads, [FSBOs](https://www.redx.com/case-studies), [FRBOs](https://www.redx.com/case-studies) — not one giant CSV you pulled three months ago.

**Mistake #2: Treating every lead type the same.** An FSBO caller script and an absentee owner script are completely different conversations. FSBO sellers are already motivated — they're just stubborn about commissions. FRBO leads skew toward landlords who might be tired of managing property. Same dialer, totally different angle.

**Mistake #3: Ignoring TCPA compliance.** San Diego isn't some rural market where nobody's paying attention. [TCPA compliant cold calling](https://www.tcpaworld.com) means scrubbing against the DNC list, documenting consent, and not blasting cell phones with auto-dialers without proper setup. One complaint can cost you.

> **Pro tip:** If you're outsourcing calls, ask whoever you hire exactly how they handle DNC scrubbing. If they fumble that answer, walk away immediately — it's not worth the exposure.

**Mistake #4: No follow-up sequence.** You connected. Great. Most sellers don't convert on call one — so if there's no CRM touchpoint after, that lead dies. Tools like [Conversion Realtor](https://conversionrealtor.com/conversion-research/real-estate-conversion-rate-benchmark) can help here — they generate intent scores and behavioral signals from lead messages, which tells you who's worth calling back first instead of just working alphabetically down a list.

Skip the shortcuts on setup. You'll save yourself weeks of bad data and frustrated callers.
