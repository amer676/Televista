## The Legal Landscape: North Carolina's Telemarketing Laws

North Carolina has its own set of telemarketing laws that you must comply with, in addition to the federal TCPA. The good news is that North Carolina's laws are relatively straightforward and easy to follow.

### The North Carolina Do Not Call List

Like most states, North Carolina has its own Do Not Call list. You must scrub your calling lists against both the state and national registries before making any calls.

### Calling Hours

The calling hours in North Carolina are the same as the federal TCPA: 8 a.m. to 9 p.m. in the recipient's time zone.

## Market Insights: Where to Focus Your Efforts in North Carolina

North Carolina has a diverse range of real estate markets, from the booming metropolis of Charlotte to the scenic mountains of Asheville. Here are a few of the top markets to consider for your cold calling efforts:

### 1. Charlotte

Charlotte is the largest city in North Carolina and a major financial hub. The city's real estate market has been on a tear in recent years, with a steady stream of new residents and a strong demand for housing.

### 2. Raleigh-Durham

The Raleigh-Durham area, also known as the Research Triangle, is a major center for technology and research. The area's strong job market and highly educated workforce make it a prime target for real estate investors.

### 3. Asheville

Asheville is a popular tourist destination known for its beautiful scenery and vibrant arts scene. The city's real estate market is competitive, but it offers a unique opportunity for investors who are interested in short-term rentals.
