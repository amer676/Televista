## What to Look for When Choosing a Cold Calling Company

Don't get burned by the wrong partner. Here's your checklist.

Your [HubSpot](https://www.hubspot.com) or [Pipedrive](https://www.pipedrive.com) setup shouldn't break when you add cold calling. Most companies claim they integrate — then you're stuck with CSV exports and manual data entry. Ask for a live demo of their API connections.

Generic B2B scripts don't work for motivated sellers. Period. The caller needs to understand equity, ARV, repair estimates — not just book meetings. [Our Televista callers](/services.html) go through 40 hours of real estate training before they touch your leads.

**3. Transparent Pricing Structure**
[Televista's flat-rate pricing with no contracts](https://televistaleadgeneration.com/best-cold-calling-companies-real-estate.html) should be the standard. But most companies bury fees. Watch for setup costs, per-minute charges, or "performance bonuses" that jack up your bill. [REVA Global starts around $1,500/mo](https://televistaleadgeneration.com/best-cold-calling-companies-real-estate.html) — make sure you know what that actually includes.

**4. Lead Scoring That Makes Sense**
Not every contact is worth the same effort. The company should rank prospects by motivation level, equity position, timeline. We've seen investors waste weeks chasing D-grade leads because their provider couldn't score properly.

> **Pro tip:** Ask to see their lead scoring criteria upfront. If they can't explain it clearly, run.

**5. Weekly Reporting (Not Monthly)**
Deal flow changes fast in real estate. Monthly reports are useless. You need weekly breakdowns of dials, connects, appointments set, and lead quality scores. Plus recorded calls for spot-checking.

**6. Dedicated Account Management**
Shared reps across 50 clients means nobody's watching your campaign. You want one person who knows your market, your criteria, your voice.
