## The 4-Step Lead Filtering System That Actually Works


<div style="background: linear-gradient(135deg, #f8fafc 0%, #e2e8f0 100%); border-radius: 12px; padding: 1.5rem 2rem; margin: 1.5rem 0; border: 1px solid #cbd5e1;">

  <p style="color: #475569; margin: 0; font-size: 0.95rem;">Key insights from this section are highlighted in the data and comparisons below.</p>
</div>


Most people download every lead from [iSpeedToLead](https://ispeedtolead.com)'s marketplace and wonder why they're calling dead ends. Wrong move.

**Step 1: Kill the obvious duds upfront.** Houses built after 2010? Skip them. These aren't distressed sellers — they're fishing for retail prices. Same with anything under 900 square feet or over 4,000. Too small screams "rental headache," too big means they've got money to fix their problems without you.

**Step 2: Focus on the 1980-2005 sweet spot.** Our Televista team ran this exact filter for a Phoenix client last quarter — his appointment rate jumped from 11% to 34% in three weeks just by tightening his year-built range. Houses from this era hit that perfect storm: old enough for repair issues, new enough that buyers actually want them.

**Step 3: Equity matters more than motivation.** Don't get fooled by sob stories with zero equity. I'd rather call someone with $80k equity and mild annoyance than desperate sellers underwater on their mortgage. Run comps first — if they owe more than 70% of value, move on.

One wholesaler I know in Tampa wastes hours chasing "highly motivated" sellers who owe $340k on $290k houses. Can't fix stupid.

**Step 4: Layer in the life events.** Divorce, job loss, inherited property — these create real urgency. [iSpeedToLead's marketplace](https://ispeedtolead.com) actually flags some of this data now (their onboarding process walks you through it). But here's what they don't tell you: recent death in family beats "thinking about selling" every single time.

> **Pro tip:** Their new AI Deal Predictor is decent for ranking leads, but don't rely on it exclusively. I've seen it rank tire-kickers as "hot prospects" because they clicked fast.

Start with manual filtering first. Get your instincts sharp. The AI can help later, but you need to understand what actually converts before letting algorithms do the thinking.

This filtering approach typically cuts your lead volume by 60% — and doubles your contract rate. Quality over quantity isn't just a cliché in wholesaling; it's survival.

## Step-By-Step: From iSpeedToLead Download to First Contract

Right, let's stop dancing around the main event. You've got your leads filtered, your account's dialed in — now comes the part where most people faceplant.

**Hour 1:** Download your batch from [iSpeedToLead's marketplace](https://ispeedtolead.com). Don't grab everything. Pick 50 leads max for your first run. Upload them to your CRM (we use [HubSpot](https://www.hubspot.com) at Televista, but [REsimpli](https://www.resimpli.com) works fine for beginners).

**Hour 1-3:** Call within 5 minutes of download. Not kidding. Speed matters more than perfect scripts. Here's your opener:

"Hi [Name], this is Mike with Capital Property Solutions. I got your information because you were looking to sell your house on [Street]. Did I catch you at a decent time?"

Pause. Let them respond. Don't rush into your pitch like an amateur.

Common objection #1: "I never requested information."

Your response: "Got it — sometimes these leads bounce around different systems. Real quick though, are you still considering selling the house, or has that ship sailed?"

**Hook them with curiosity, not pressure.**

**Hours 4-6:** First callbacks. Most people quit here. Don't. Your follow-up script:

"Hey [Name], Mike again from yesterday about the house on [Street]. I know you mentioned you weren't quite ready yet, but I wanted to run something by you real quick. We've got a client specifically looking in your neighborhood — would it make sense to grab a quick 15 minutes this week?"

**Day 2-5:** Text sequences. Our [Televista](/services.html) team tested this across 200+ leads last quarter — SMS converts 3x better than voicemail:

"Hi [Name], Mike from Capital Property Solutions. Still thinking about that house situation? No pressure — just wanted you to have my number. Text me back if you want to chat: [Your Number]"

> **Key Stat:** 73% of contracts come from leads contacted 5+ times

**Week 1:** The money call. When they finally engage (and they will), flip to consultant mode:

"Walk me through what's happening with the house. What's driving the decision to sell?"

Listen. Actually listen. Don't interrupt with your wholesale pitch. Find their pain point first.

Typical responses:
- "Need to relocate for work" → Timeline pressure
- "Can't afford repairs" → Cash flow problem  
- "Inherited property" → Emotional/logistical burden

Match your solution to their specific problem. "Based on what you're telling me, it sounds like you need this handled quickly and with zero headaches. We buy houses exactly as-is — no repairs, no showings, no waiting on financing. Would it help if I came by Thursday to take a look?"

**Week 2-3:** Property visit and contract. Bring your calculator, not your ego. Run comps on [PropStream](https://www.propstream.com), subtract repair costs, leave room for your fee and buyer's profit.

Offer 60-70% of ARV minus repairs. They say no? That's fine. Counter with: "What number would work for you?"

Sometimes they surprise you.

One Televista client in Phoenix just closed a deal using this exact workflow — went from download to signed contract in 18 days. Made $8,500 on the assignment.

The secret sauce isn't the script. It's the persistence most people can't stomach.

## The Follow-Up Sequences That Turn Cold Leads into Contracts

Most people call once, maybe send a text, then wonder why their [iSpeedToLead](https://ispeedtolead.com) seller leads aren't converting. Wrong approach entirely.

**Here's the sequence that works.** Day 1: Call within 2 hours of lead download (speed to lead strategy matters more than your script). No answer? Text immediately: "Hi [Name], tried calling about your [Address] property. When's a good time to chat?" Day 3: Second call. Day 5: Email with a subject line that doesn't scream "wholesaler" — something like "Quick question about Maple Street."

[iSpeedToLead's onboarding resources](https://onboarding.ispeedtolead.com) include their "Follow Up Sequences For More Contracts" guide, but honestly? Their timing's too aggressive. We've tested both approaches with our [Televista](/services.html) clients and the modified version wins every time.

The sweet spot is 7 touches over 21 days:
- **Touch 1:** Phone call (immediate)
- **Touch 2:** Text (same day)  
- **Touch 3:** Phone call (day 3)
- **Touch 4:** Email (day 5)
- **Touch 5:** Phone call (day 9)
- **Touch 6:** Text check-in (day 14)
- **Touch 7:** Final call (day 21)

> **Pro tip:** Don't pitch on the first call. Just ask if they're still considering selling. Gets way better response rates.

**The scripts matter less than consistency.** One Televista client went from 1 contract per month to 4 just by following this exact sequence. Took him six weeks to see the pattern, but once he did — game over.

Most motivated sellers real estate deals happen between touches 3-5. They need time to process that someone actually wants their problem property, especially if they downloaded it from [iSpeedToLead's leads marketplace](https://ispeedtolead.com).

Skip the robotic CRM automation (looking at you, [REsimpli](https://www.resimpli.com) users). Personal touches win deals.

## Disposition Strategies: Getting Your Contracts Sold Fast

Most people think getting the contract signed is the finish line. Wrong.

That's mile marker 7 of a 10-mile race. Now comes the part that separates weekend warriors from actual wholesalers — moving that paper fast enough to keep your assignment fees intact.

**Your buyer list is everything.** Start building it before you even get your first contract under agreement. We track this at [Televista](/services.html) religiously — wholesalers with 50+ active cash buyers close 73% of their contracts. Under 20 buyers? You're looking at maybe 31%.

Hit up [BiggerPockets](https://www.biggerpockets.com), local REI meetups, Facebook groups. But here's the move nobody talks about — cold call investors who just closed deals. [PropStream](https://www.propstream.com) shows you who bought cash in your market last 90 days. Call them directly.

**Pricing your assignments right matters more than your contract terms.** I've seen too many newbies get greedy. You found a $180K house, got it under contract at $140K, thinking you'll pocket $25K on assignment. Nope. Price at $155K, take your $15K, and move on to the next deal. Speed beats greed every time.

The closing process? Don't wing it. [DealSpeed](https://onboarding.ispeedtolead.com) offers dispo services that handle this headache (honestly worth considering for your first few deals). But if you're going DIY, connect your buyer with your title company immediately. Not next week — same day you sign the assignment agreement.

One of our Televista clients learned this the hard way last month. Had a sweet $18K assignment lined up, waited four days to introduce buyer to title. Deal fell apart over inspection issues that could've been sorted day one.

> **Pro tip:** Build relationships with 2-3 title companies that actually understand assignments. Most don't.
