The Maryland Problem

Baltimore Vacants and DC Spillover — Two Markets, One State

Maryland's real estate market is split between Baltimore's high-vacancy urban core and the DC suburbs where prices climb fast. Both sides create opportunity, but reaching sellers before they list or get cold-called by competitors requires dedicated, daily outbound effort.

01

Baltimore's Vacancy Crisis

Baltimore has tens of thousands of vacant and abandoned properties. Many owners are willing to sell but don't know where to start. Without callers working these lists daily, those motivated sellers end up going to tax sale or another investor.

02

DC Metro Price Pressure

In Montgomery and Prince George's counties, rising prices push homeowners into financial strain. Callers trained on these suburban dynamics can identify sellers facing rate resets, divorce, or job relocation — before they list with an agent.

03

Multi-State Investor Competition

Maryland's position between DC, Virginia, and Pennsylvania means investors from four states compete for the same deals. Speed to contact is everything — and that requires a full-time caller, not weekend dialing sessions.

FAQ

Maryland Cold Calling Questions

How does Televista generate real estate leads in Maryland?

We deploy trained cold callers who work your specific Maryland zip codes and counties. They contact homeowners from curated lists — including pre-foreclosure, probate, absentee owner, and vacant property data — and identify motivated sellers ready to have a conversation about selling their property.

Which Maryland cities and markets do you cover?

We cover every Maryland market. Our most active campaigns run in Baltimore, Annapolis, Silver Spring, Columbia, Frederick, Rockville, and Prince George's County. You choose the exact zip codes and counties you want to target.

Is cold calling legal in Maryland for real estate investors?

Yes. Cold calling is legal in Maryland when done in compliance with the TCPA and Maryland's Telephone Solicitations Act. Televista uses only human callers (no robocalls), honors the Do Not Call registry, calls within legal hours, and follows one-to-one consent protocols to keep your business fully compliant.
